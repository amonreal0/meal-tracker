# Albóndigas in chipotle tomato broth

Makes **2 large servings** (3 with rice/tortillas). Eat with rice, tortillas, or crusty
bread; add zucchini or spinach to the broth if you want vegetables in it.

## Ingredients

### Meatballs
- 1 lb ground beef, pork, or beef/pork mix
- ½ small onion, finely chopped or grated
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 egg
- ½ cup breadcrumbs
- ¼ cup chopped cilantro
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp dried oregano

### Chipotle tomato broth
- 1 tbsp olive or neutral oil
- ½ small onion, chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1–2 chipotles in adobo, chopped
- 1 tbsp adobo sauce from the can
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- ~1 cup chicken broth (was 1½ — too soupy last time; go lighter)
- ½ tsp salt, then adjust
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- Lime juice, to finish
- Cilantro, to finish

### To serve (pick one)
- Cooked white rice / tortillas / crusty bread

## Method
1. Mix all meatball ingredients in a bowl. Don't overwork it.
2. Form into about 12 meatballs.
3. In a pot, heat oil. Sauté onion 3–4 min.
4. Add garlic, chipotle, adobo, cumin, oregano. Cook 30 sec.
5. Add crushed tomatoes and broth. Bring to a simmer.
6. Gently drop in the meatballs.
7. Cover and simmer 18–22 min, until cooked through.
8. Finish with lime and cilantro.

**Leftover move:** reheat and eat as tacos with cilantro, lime, and maybe avocado.
