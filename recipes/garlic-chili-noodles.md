# Garlic chili noodles with ground pork

Makes **2 servings**. Fast Friday-ish meal.

## Ingredients
- 8 oz noodles
- ½ lb ground pork
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp ginger, minced
- 3 scallions, sliced (separate white and green parts)
- 2 tbsp chili crisp or chili oil
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil (optional)
- 1 tsp sugar (optional — balances the sauce, not for sweetness; can skip)
- ½ cup chicken broth or water
- 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 1 tbsp water (for a glossy, clingy sauce — skippable)
- Optional: 2 cups spinach
- Optional: cucumber slices on the side

## Method
1. Cook noodles. Save ½ cup noodle water, then drain.
2. Mix sauce: soy, rice vinegar, sesame oil, chili crisp, broth/water, cornstarch slurry.
3. Brown ground pork over medium-high heat.
4. Add garlic, ginger, scallion whites. Cook 1 min.
5. Add sauce, simmer until glossy, 1–2 min.
6. Add noodles and toss. Add noodle water if needed.
7. Add spinach at the end if using.
8. Top with scallion greens and more chili crisp.

**Leftover move:** reheat in a pan with a splash of water; add a fried egg to make it feel new.

> Note: the **cornstarch is only for this recipe** — it thickens the sauce so it coats the
> noodles instead of pooling. Skip it and the noodles are still good, just less saucy.
