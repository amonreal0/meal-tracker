# Profile

## The person
- One person, lives in NYC.
- Cooks **3 dinners/week**, wants this to be a sustainable long-term rotation.
- Grew up in Mexico; loves salsas and fried food.
- Reasonably proficient cook, but prefers recipes that **don't take too long**.
- Wants to eat more **salads** and more **fruit**.
- No breakfast or lunch at home on weekdays. Usually not cooking on weekends either, though
  occasionally open to it — don't overbuy for weekend meals.
- Budget is not a major concern.
- Groceries come via delivery (**FreshDirect** is primary). Single-person household, so
  **spoilage and over-buying are the main enemy.**

## Standing shopping preferences
- **Flowers ~once a month.** FreshDirect included flowers as a first-order gift (2026-06-07)
  and he liked it — add a flower bunch to roughly one cart per month. Last added: 2026-06-07.
  When building a cart, check whether it's been ~a month since the last flowers and offer to add them.
- Reorder favorites: FreshDirect raspberries and avocados were standouts (see `pantry.md`).

## Equipment
- Air fryer
- Instant Pot
- Stovetop / skillet / wok

## Hard preferences
- **No sweet meals**, especially meat with a sweet sauce — avoid BBQ, mole, honey-glazed, etc.
- Likes: spicy, savory, acidic, crispy, fresh-and-rich.

## Taste profile (derived from what he picked)
- **Spicy / savory / acidic:** chipotle, chile, salsa, harissa, spicy mayo, garlic-chili,
  artichoke-jalapeño, chili crisp, chopped salads.
- **Crispy things:** fish tacos, crispy tofu, crispy chickpeas, crispy rice, BLT, chile relleno.
- **Fresh + rich combinations:** avocado, arugula, mozzarella, tomato, cucumber salad,
  creamy/spicy sauces.
- **Legumes are welcome:** chana masala, lentil soup, crispy chickpeas.
- **Sandwiches count as a real dinner** — important low-effort, anti-spoilage category.

## The 5 dinner families (the planning skeleton)
Each week, pull roughly one from a few of these. Default: 1 saucy/stew + 1 crispy + 1 fast
noodle/bowl or salad/sandwich, plus leftover nights and a no-cook backup.

1. **Saucy / stewy comfort** — cook once, eat twice. (Albóndigas, lentil soups, chana masala,
   carne con papas, tortilla soup, bean stews.) Best as the Mon/Tue cook.
2. **Crispy / air-fryer** — scratches the fried itch, usually best fresh not as leftovers.
   (Fish tacos, crispy tofu, crispy chickpeas, cutlets, wings, flautas, chile relleno.) Midweek.
3. **Big salads that are actually dinner** — for the "more salad/fruit" goal. (Italian chopped,
   chicken-spinach-avocado, crispy chickpea, harissa chicken cucumber, BLT salad.) Good when
   there's leftover protein.
4. **Noodles / rice / bowls** — fast, high-satisfaction. (Garlic chili noodles, crispy rice
   bowl w/ spicy tuna, mapo tofu, kimchi fried rice, Thai basil chicken.) Friday / lazy night.
5. **Sandwich / no-cook / low-cook** — single-person essential. (Turkey + artichoke-jalapeño,
   salami-mozz-tomato-arugula, BLT + spicy mayo, tuna melt, tortas.) Sunday / annoyed-to-cook night.

## Anti-spoilage shopping rule
Don't buy a giant "healthy haul." Buy ingredients that **overlap across the week's recipes**,
and within produce aim for roughly:
- **one delicate green** (spinach / arugula)
- **one sturdy vegetable** (cabbage, etc.)
- **one herb** (cilantro *or* parsley, rarely both)
- **one fast-spoiling fruit** (berries, grapes, mango)
- **one lasting fruit** (apples, oranges, mandarins)
- avocados: 2 max, not 4.

## "Use it up" order
- **First 2 days:** berries, avocado, fresh seafood, delicate herbs.
- **Mid-week:** spinach, cucumber, cooked leftovers.
- **End of week:** cabbage, tortillas, apples/citrus, tofu, jarred sauces.

## Leftovers & portions
- Recipes default to 2 servings. He tolerates roughly one leftover night per cook; don't
  plan 4 nights of the same thing.
- **Portions run generous for him — lean toward less, not more.** Week of 2026-06-07 was too
  much food.
- **Treat starch sides (rice, couscous, naan, tortillas) as optional, and don't double up on
  carbs in one meal.** He often skips them; the mains are filling on their own.
- FreshDirect sometimes throws in free produce; he turns it into salads, which fills more of
  the week than planned. Don't over-buy produce assuming every night needs a planned side.
